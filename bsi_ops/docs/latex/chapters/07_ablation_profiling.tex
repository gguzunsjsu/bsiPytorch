\chapter{Ablation Studies and Profiling}
\section{Scope Ablation}
The contrast between \texttt{scope=all} and \texttt{scope=attention} is one of the most important ablations in the entire study. It shows that the current BSI regime is not uniformly compatible with all linear subgraphs. Attention-only replacement is clearly more robust. This provides strong motivation for later layer-aware or subgraph-aware policies.

\section{Larger-Model Sensitivity}
The 30B model was used primarily as a sensitivity study. The observed pattern was that attention-only replacement remained relatively close to the dense baseline, MLP-only replacement degraded more substantially, and \texttt{scope=all} collapsed. The conclusion is that MLP layers are the dominant quality bottleneck under the current fixed-bit BSI regime.

\section{Negative Results from Development}
Several exploratory ideas did not survive into the stable path:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
\item aggressive runtime query repacking increased build time too much,
\item some low-level layout changes broke end-to-end correctness,
\item duplicate storage of legacy and experimental tensors inflated model size until corrected,
\item lower bitwidth improved speed but degraded quality beyond the preferred operating point.
\end{itemize}
These negative results are important because they define the real design constraints rather than an idealized design space.

\section{Nsight Compute Findings}
The most important profiler snapshot came from the same-bit tensor-core kernel on the 6.7B-like FC1 shape $Q=512, R=16384, D=4096$. The critical findings were low achieved occupancy, low eligible warps per scheduler, high no-eligible stall time, and substantial excessive shared/global transaction overhead. The key conclusion is that the current kernel is not primarily DRAM-bandwidth bound. The more fundamental limitation is scheduler and access inefficiency.
